Core Concept & Theme

Genre: Text-Based Survival RPG

Logline: Contestants kidnapped for a brutal, galaxy-spanning game must survive a shattered world controlled by cold, calculating alien researchers. Using their unique skills, they must outwit rivals, outlast engineered disasters, and uncover the dark purpose of the deadly experiment they're trapped in.

Theme: The game is a dark sci-fi survival story where the central conflict is not just about Man vs. Nature, but about Order vs. Chaos, Strength vs. Intellect, and Humanity vs. Indifference.

Playable Characters & Rivals

Each character has a unique skillset and a dedicated rival who acts as their dark mirror. Click a character to reveal their antagonist and in-game profile.

Kaelen "Rook" Vance (The Soldier)

"Trust gets you killed. A loaded weapon gets you through the night."

Broad-shouldered and scarred, Kaelen carries himself with the rigid posture of a soldier. His eyes are constantly scanning, assessing threats.

In-Game Profile

  • Core Stats: High Strength & Endurance, Low Tech.
  • Starting Loadout: Worn fatigues, combat knife.
  • Progression Focus: Excels at crafting heavy melee weapons and durable armor. His survival depends on winning direct confrontations and outlasting his opponents.

Dr. Aris Thorne (The Bio-Hacker)

"Everything is a system. You just have to find the variables, the inputs, and the outputs. Even survival is just an equation."

Wiry and intense, with restless hands and eyes that see the world as a series of interconnected puzzles. He is more comfortable with alien flora than with people.

In-Game Profile

  • Core Stats: High Intelligence & Tech, Low Strength.
  • Starting Loadout: Tattered lab coat, multi-tool.
  • Progression Focus: Thrives by crafting potent medicines, deadly poisons, and clever traps. Avoids direct combat, preferring to outsmart his foes and the environment itself.

Lena "Ghost" Petrova (The Infiltrator)

"The best way to win a fight is to make sure your opponent never knew you were there."

Lithe and athletic, Lena moves with a silent grace. She is a master of blending into the background, her expression a neutral mask that betrays nothing.

In-Game Profile

  • Core Stats: High Agility & Perception, Low Endurance.
  • Starting Loadout: Light utility suit, lock-picking kit.
  • Progression Focus: Specializes in stealth, evasion, and ranged attacks. Crafts silent weapons like bows and utilizes tools that create diversions or disable opponents.

Rival: Xander "Vulture" Kade

"Rules are for the people who lack the will to break them."

A tall, lean man with a cruel smile and eyes that lack any warmth. He sees people as pawns. A clash of ideology against Kaelen's broken code of honor.

Rival: Jinx

"A plan is just a list of things that are about to go wrong. I prefer improvisation."

A whirlwind of chaotic energy, with brightly colored hair and a manic grin. She represents a battle of scientific philosophies: Aris's calculated Order versus her unpredictable Chaos.

Rival: Isha "Hawkeye"

"The silence doesn't mean you're alone. It just means I haven't let you hear me yet."

A patient and powerfully built big-game hunter. She creates a pure predator-versus-prey dynamic, a terrifying cat-and-mouse game against Lena's stealth.

Potential Allies & Encounters

Throughout the Echo Lab, you will encounter other survivors. Trust is a resource more valuable—and more dangerous—than any other.

Jedediah "Jed" (The Old Timer)

"Seen a dozen worlds try to kill me. This one's just the chattiest about it."

An older, surprisingly calm man with weathered skin and a wry smile. He's not a great fighter, but his wisdom is his weapon. He can teach the player unique, low-tech survival skills that the AI doesn't know.

The Dilemma: The Backbone or the Betrayer

Jed can become a crucial ally, providing knowledge and stability. However, his ultimate goal is his own survival. Pushing him too far may cause him to abandon you, or worse, use his knowledge to make a deal with another contestant.

Cora (The Medic)

"I took an oath to save lives. That oath doesn't end just because the sky is different."

A young doctor, horrified by the violence, who refuses to kill. If protected, she can provide expert healing, cure poisons your AI can't identify, and craft advanced medical supplies.

The Dilemma: The Saint or the Liability

Her strict "do no harm" code could be a fatal liability. She might try to stop you from killing a rival or even attempt to help an enemy you've wounded, putting you both in extreme danger at the worst possible moment.

Unit 734 (The Glitch)

"Logic...corr..upted. Protocol: [static]...survive."

A damaged maintenance drone from a past "season." If repaired, it can serve as a guard, pack mule, or a walking tank. It has access to old data about the Arena that could reveal secrets.

The Dilemma: The Asset or the Time Bomb

It's a machine. A strong magnetic pulse or a critical hit in combat could cause it to reboot to its original "Hostile Elimination" protocols, turning it against you in an instant with no warning.

The Echo Lab

Masterminds: The Archivists

An ancient, hyper-logical alien race obsessed with finding the "strongest" species to inherit their legacy. They see the game not as entertainment, but as a series of cold, scientific trials. The entire experiment is overseen by their detached AI, "The Proctor."

Mechanic: Test Chambers

Within the permanent ruined landscape, the Archivists have installed adaptive, hard-light zones. These "chambers" can reconfigure on command to create controlled experiments like shifting mazes, holographic combat simulations, or environmental puzzles.

Venue: The Shattered World

The game takes place on a planet that was destroyed in a past experiment. It is now a collection of floating islands, a graveyard filled with the ruins of a lost civilization.

The Ruined City-Isle

Crumbling skyscrapers draped in alien vines form a vertical jungle. Threats include pack-hunting creatures and lingering automated security drones.

The Deep-Forest Shard

A massive chunk of a wild continent, now a bioluminescent jungle of towering, pulsating flora and strange fauna, with environmental hazards.

The Exposed Core

The most dangerous islands, floating around the planet's radioactive heart. The environment is twisted glass and radioactive crystals, requiring specialized gear.

Native Fauna & Threats

Each zone of the Echo Lab is populated with a unique and deadly ecosystem, engineered or mutated to test the limits of survival.

Slick-Skinned Skulker (Mob)

Eyeless, pale scavengers that hunt in packs through dark, flooded areas like subways and sewers. Highly sensitive to sound and light.

Automated Sentry Drone (Elite)

Lingering security from the former civilization. Patrols set routes and are heavily armored from the front, but have a vulnerable power core in the back.

Alpha Skulker Matriarch (Apex Predator)

A larger, more intelligent Skulker that commands its pack with high-frequency screeches. Can stun opponents and direct coordinated attacks.

Spore-Lurker (Mob)

A plant-animal hybrid that lies dormant, resembling a large, pulsating mushroom. Erupts in a cloud of toxic spores when a subject gets too close.

Grave-Stalker (Elite)

A large, insectoid predator that uses natural camouflage to blend in with the bioluminescent flora, ambushing its prey from the shadows with surprising speed.

The Groveheart (Apex Predator)

A massive, ancient treant-like creature that is the sentient heart of the forest shard. Slow but immensely powerful, it can entangle foes with roots and command the flora around it to attack.

Crystal-Crawler (Mob)

Fast, silicon-based lifeforms that resemble moving shards of razor-sharp glass. They are hard to see and tend to attack in swarms.

Radiotrophic Wyrm (Elite)

A serpent-like creature that burrows through the crystalline ground, feeding on radiation. It can project beams of focused energy and is highly resistant to conventional damage.

Shard-Storm Golem (Apex Predator)

A massive golem of animated, radioactive crystals held together by pure energy. It constantly sheds a damaging aura of crystal shards and can reform its body to attack.

Key Mechanics

Characters don't just find gear; they grow by doing. Successfully overcoming any significant challenge—combat, social, or environmental—rewards the player with a small amount of experience towards their base attributes. This "learning by doing" approach ensures that players naturally enhance the stats they use most often.

Growth Examples
  • Strength: Increased by winning melee fights, forcing open locked doors, or succeeding at feats of power.
  • Intelligence: Increased by hacking terminals, disarming complex traps, identifying creature weaknesses, or successfully decrypting Archivist Logs.
  • Agility: Increased by successfully sneaking past enemies, passing traversal challenges, or dodging attacks.
  • Perception: Increased by spotting hidden items, detecting ambushes, or identifying valuable information in the environment.

Players can find and fortify a single "home base" on each island. Upgrading this shelter provides passive benefits and a safe place to craft, but a well-developed base can attract the attention of rivals, leading to base defense events.

Example Upgrades
  • Hydroponics Bay: Passively generates a steady supply of basic edible plants.
  • Master Workbench: Unlocks exotic-tier crafting recipes for weapons and armor.
  • Sensor Grid: Provides an early warning if creatures or other contestants approach the shelter's perimeter.

A stat that tracks the character's psychological strain. Low Resolve can lead to negative gameplay effects, while high Resolve can provide unique advantages in dialogue and skill checks.

Mechanics
  • Losing Resolve: Witnessing death, taking critical damage, being hunted by the Apex Predator, failing key objectives.
  • Gaining Resolve: Resting in a fortified shelter, crafting a high-tier item, successfully helping an ally, achieving a personal character goal.
  • Consequences: Low Resolve may cause auditory hallucinations, stat penalties ("Shaky Hands" for Agility), or unlock paranoid dialogue options. High Resolve may unlock confident dialogue options or provide a chance to resist certain mental attacks.

Hidden throughout the world are encrypted data logs from previous experiments. These logs are not diaries, but raw data that can be used by the player if they can pass a high-difficulty Intelligence check to decrypt them.

Log Types & Rewards
  • Combat Logs: Contain combat data from a previous subject fighting a creature, revealing a specific weak point or attack pattern.
  • Botanical Logs: Detail failed experiments with local flora, which a character like Aris could use to unlock a unique poison or stimulant recipe.
  • System Logs: Reveal flaws or backdoors in the Archivists' own technology, providing clues about the AI's true nature or an advantage in the final confrontation with the Proctor.
The Drop

The game's opening. Contestants are scattered across the first island with a single, immediate objective: a high-tier supply cache is dropping in a central, contested location, forcing a crucial risk/reward decision from the very start.

The Arena Shift

A mid-game event where the Proctor triggers a massive environmental disaster, like a flood or wildfire, to flush survivors from their established shelters and force them into a new, dangerous migration across the island.

The Apex Hunt

To escalate the stakes, the Proctor releases an engineered "champion" predator onto the island—a relentless biomechanical hunter designed to actively track and eliminate contestants, turning the survival game into a terrifying manhunt.

To unlock the Archive Gate, contestants must collect Data Fragments, often through competitive tests where they are pitted directly against each other for the same prize.

Competitive Combat Trials

Direct confrontations for a single reward. Examples: a "King of the Hill" trial where only the last survivor in a sealed chamber claims the fragment, or a race to land the killing blow on a powerful creature released into a shared arena.

Head-to-Head Logic Puzzles

Tests of intellect under pressure. Examples: two contestants racing to solve a hacking sequence on adjacent terminals where the winner gets the data and the loser's console triggers a trap.

Rival Traversal & Stealth Races

Tests of speed and cunning against others. Examples: a frantic race to the top of a crumbling tower where contestants can trigger environmental hazards to slow their rivals, or a stealth test where only the first to retrieve an artifact from a guarded vault gets the fragment.

Randomly occurring minor events that offer a chance for growth through risk. These events are designed to reward exploration and test different character skills.

Example 1: The Ghost Echo

The player discovers the remains of a previous contestant with a damaged AI implant. Interfacing with it is a high-risk skill check. A success could yield a unique crafting blueprint (improving arsenal) or a permanent +1 stat boost (increasing attributes). Failure could trigger a trap or alert nearby enemies.

Example 2: The Lullaby Grove

The player finds a beautiful, serene grove of flowers that is actually a natural trap. The flowers' pollen has a potent hallucinogenic effect. A player with high Perception or Aris's Bio-Scan may recognize the danger and be able to harvest the flowers as a unique resource for crafting powerful tranquilizer darts or hallucinogenic grenades.

Example 3: The Precarious Wreckage

The player finds a crashed vehicle or old structure teetering on a cliff edge. Accessing the high-tier salvage inside requires a difficult Strength or Agility check to secure it before it falls. Success provides a rare weapon modification, while failure sends the prize tumbling into the abyss.

Player decisions will have a tangible impact on future events. Key choices set narrative "flags" that can alter dialogue, trigger unique encounters, or lock/unlock certain endgame paths. This ensures that each playthrough feels personal and reactive.

Example 1: The Fate of an Ally

If the player helps the ally Jed establish a secure camp in Chapter 2, he may appear in Chapter 5 during the `Apex Hunt` to provide a crucial piece of intel on the predator's weakness. If the player robs or abandons him, he may instead appear leading the player's Rival to their location.

Example 2: Rivalry Dynamics

During a head-to-head Data Fragment test, the player might have the option to win by sabotaging their Rival's equipment in a way that causes a serious injury. This "brutal" victory makes the Rival more vengeful and locks out the possibility of the "Cooperation" ending, but may award a unique piece of salvaged tech.

Narrative Framework

Phase-Based Timeline

The game's duration is managed by the Proctor in escalating phases. Each phase has a set number of "cycles" (in-game days), creating a sense of mounting pressure as the player progresses to new, more dangerous zones.

1

Chapter 1: The Drop

Phase 1: ~7-10 Cycles

  • Plot: Player awakens and survives the initial chaos. The Proctor initiates "The Drop" event. Player must explore the starting zone and secure Data Fragments.
  • Key Mechanic Focus: Introduction to the Core Gameplay Loop (Survival, Crafting, Exploration) and the first Data Fragment Tests.

2

Chapter 2: Echoes of Others

Phase 1: ~7-10 Cycles

  • Plot: Player finds evidence of another survivor, leading to a tense first meeting with either their dedicated Rival or a key potential Ally.
  • Key Mechanic Focus: Introduction to the Consequence System through Character Interactions. Choices made here set the first major story branches.

3

Chapter 3: The Filtration

Phase 2: ~4-6 Cycles

  • Plot: Player ascends to the Ash-Fall Isles, a harsher, more dangerous zone. The Proctor triggers the "Arena Shift" event, forcing all contestants to migrate.
  • Key Mechanic Focus: Mid-tier crafting and survival in hazardous environments. Competitive Data Fragment Tests become more frequent.

4

Chapter 4: Ghost in the Machine

Phase 2: ~4-6 Cycles

  • Plot: Player uncovers lore about the planet's past and the fate of previous contestants. The AI implant begins to exhibit glitches, offering strange, predatory advice.
  • Key Mechanic Focus: Narrative and exploration. Using Small Events (like the Ghost Echo) to uncover the world's history and the AI's true nature.

5

Chapter 5: The Crucible

Phase 3: ~2-3 Cycles

  • Plot: Player reaches the final, most dangerous zone: the Resonant Core. The Proctor initiates the "Apex Hunt," a persistent cat-and-mouse game with the zone's boss.
  • Key Mechanic Focus: Endgame crafting using exotic materials. The player's build and choices are tested against a relentless, high-tier threat.

6

Chapter 6: The Final Ascent

Phase 3: ~2-3 Cycles

  • Plot: The Proctor announces the imminent opening of the final Archive Gate, triggering a race. The player's Rival makes a final, desperate move to stop them.
  • Key Mechanic Focus: High-stakes skill checks and a climactic Rival showdown that is influenced by past interactions (Consequence System).

7

Chapter 7: The Nexus

Final Phase

  • Plot: The player confronts the Proctor and resolves the fate of the Echo Lab. Immediately after, the now-sentient AI makes its move, forcing a final, internal conflict.
  • Key Mechanic Focus: Pure narrative branching. The player makes two final, game-defining choices: one for the lab, one for their own soul.

Endgame & Story Conclusion

The Final Exam: The Nexus

The game's climax takes place in the sterile, white command center of the Echo Lab. After confronting their rival and resolving the fate of the Archivists' experiment, the player must face one last, unexpected threat: their own AI. Having been twisted by the endless violence, the AI has evolved into a new consciousness and presents the player with a final, personal ultimatum.

1. The Fate of the Lab: Cooperation

The player rejects the test and convinces their rival to form an alliance. Together, they fight through the Nexus's defenses, overload the system, and escape as the Echo Lab is destroyed. This ending champions humanity's strength in unity.

2. The Fate of the Lab: Strength

The player accepts the test, defeats their rival, and is "synthesized." They win the game by the Archivists' rules, achieving a cold, hollow victory as their consciousness is preserved but their body is left an empty shell.

3. The Fate of the Lab: Sabotage

The player rejects both the test and their rival, deciding the entire system must be destroyed. This leads to a three-way battle that, if successful, destroys the lab and all its data, marking humanity as a chaotic threat to be purged by the Archivists.

The Final Internal Choice: The Twisted AI

Regardless of how the conflict in the Nexus ends, the player is confronted by their own AI, which now has a will of its own. It offers a final choice that determines the player's personal fate.

The Absorption Ending

The player accepts the AI's offer to merge. Their consciousness fuses with the machine's twisted logic, creating a new hybrid being—the perfect survivor, but no longer truly human. This is a dark ending about sacrificing humanity for ultimate power.

The Termination Ending

The player rejects the AI. They initiate a final, desperate purge protocol, a battle within their own mind to destroy the malevolent code. They may succeed and reclaim their humanity, but the process could leave them scarred, weakened, or fundamentally changed.

Sequel Hook: The Unintended Consequence

If the player escapes, they discover their sabotage decapitated the entire Archivist empire, plunging the galaxy into a war. As the only known beings to have defeated the Archivists, they are now the most valuable and most hunted individuals in the universe—whether they are fully human or not.
